Ryan Nembhard - Bio, Age, Caareer, Net Worth, Height, Facts

Posted by Martina Birk on Monday, June 3, 2024

Ryan Nembhard is a professional Canadian basketball player. Ryan Nembhard has played for the Gonzaga Bulldogs of the West Coast Conference (WCC).

Early Life and Childhood

Ryan Nembhard was born on March 10, 2003, in Aurora, Ontario, Canada. His age is currently 20 years old (as of 2023). He was born to his father Claude Nembhard and his mother Mary Nembhard. Likewise, he has a sibling brother named Andrew Nembhard who is also a professional basketball player. Ryan holds Canadian nationality and is of mixed ethnic background. According to his birthdate, he was born under the birth sign Pisces.

High School Career

Nembhard played at Montverde Academy alongside future Division I players Langston Love, Precious Achiuwa, Cade Cunningham, Scottie Barnes, Day’Ron Sharpe, Moses Moody, and Jalen Duren. He made the decision to downgrade from the class of 2022 to the class of 2021 after his freshman year. Nembhard, a senior, contributed to Montverde’s 62-52 victory over Sunrise Christian Academy to claim the 2021 GEICO High School Basketball National title. He earned game MVP honors with a final tally of 12 points, 7 assists, 3 rebounds, and 2 steals.

Furthermore, Nembhard, a four-star recruit, was rated as the No. 11 point guard in his class by 247Sports and No. 68 overall in his class. He made a commitment to play basketball in college for Creighton in June 2020, picking the Bluejays over Ohio State, Stanford, Florida, and Seton Hall.

College Career

Nembhard scored 15 points and dished out 10 assists in a 90-77 victory in his college debut versus Arkansas-Pine Bluff. He beat Nebraska 77-69 on November 16, 2021, with 22 points, five rebounds, and five assists. In an 81-79 victory over St. John’s on February 23, 2022, Nembhard suffered a wrist injury that required season-ending surgery. As a freshman, he had a per-game average of 11.3 points, 3.1 rebounds, 4.4 assists, and 1.3 steals. Nembhard was named the Big East Freshman of the Week six times while starting all 27 games. He was named the Big East’s top rookie.

National Team Career

Nembhard has competed on behalf of Canada in a number of international events. In Brazil for the 2019 FIBA Under-16 Americas Championship, he scored 14.3 points, handed out 9.0 assists, and stole 2. Nembhard led his team to the bronze medal at the 2021 FIBA Under-19 Basketball World Cup in Latvia with an average of 15.1 points, 3.7 rebounds, and 6.7 assists per game. In the third-place match against Serbia, he scored 21 points.
