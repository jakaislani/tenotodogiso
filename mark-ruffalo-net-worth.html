What is Mark Ruffalo’s Net Worth and Salary?

Mark Ruffalo, an American actor, director, producer, and screenwriter, is worth $35 million. His successful and celebrated career in Hollywood includes notable roles in film and stage productions, earning him heaps of praise from critics.

Early Life and Education

Mark Alan Ruffalo was born on November 22, 1967 in Kenosha, Wisconsin, to working-class parents of Italian and French-Canadian descent. He grew up with two sisters and a brother. In 2008, his brother was found dead in front of his house from a bullet wound to the back of the head. The murder has never been solved.

Ruffalo struggled with undiagnosed dyslexia and ADHD during his school years, which made it challenging for him to learn. He attended Catholic and progressive grade schools before moving to Virginia Beach and San Diego with his family. He was an avid wrestler in high school and began his journey into acting during his teen years. Ruffalo studied acting at the Stella Adler Conservatory in Los Angeles.

Mark Ruffalo’s Career Highlights

Mark Ruffalo started his career co-founding Orpheus Theater Company and working as a bartender. He gained notoriety in the 2000s with films like In the Cut, Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind, and Zodiac.

Ruffalo’s breakout role came in Shutter Island, earning him a Best Supporting Actor Oscar nomination. He’s well-known for playing detectives in films like Now You See Me, Collateral, and Zodiac.

Ruffalo joined the Marvel Cinematic Universe in 2012 as Dr. Bruce Banner/The Hulk, appearing in several films including The Avengers, Avengers: Endgame, and becoming part of the highest-grossing film in cinematic history.

Outside of Marvel, Ruffalo has starred in plays like A Normal Heart and films like Spotlight, You Can Count On Me, and Foxcatcher.

Mark Ruffalo’s Award Winning Career

Mark Ruffalo’s career has been decorated with numerous awards, including a New Generation Award from the Los Angeles Film Critics Association and an Award for Best Supporting Actor from the New York Film Critics Circle. His role in Spotlight earned him a Screen Actors Guild Award, a Satellite Award, and an Independent Spirit Award. Ruffalo also won a Primetime Emmy Award for his work in A Normal Heart. He has been nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor three times.

Mark Ruffalo’s Political Views

Mark Ruffalo, known for his outspoken political views, is against fracking and has expressed concerns about oil companies eyeing his land for future development. He has also questioned the “official story” surrounding the 9/11 attacks and the suspicious manner in which the World Trade Center buildings fell. Ruffalo is a vocal opponent of “far-right nationalism” and has urged NBC to stop hiring conservative commentators.

Mark Ruffalo’s Marriage to Sunrise Coigney

In the year 2000, Mark Ruffalo married Sunrise Coigney after being rejected once before. Coigney has been credited with helping Ruffalo during a low point in his career. The couple has three children and splits their time between Manhattan and the Catskill Mountains in Sullivan County.

Mark Ruffalo’s Health Scare

Shortly after marrying Coigney, Ruffalo discovered he had a benign brain tumor. He recorded a video message for his unborn child, as Coigney was pregnant at the time. The surgery to remove the tumor was successful, but it left Ruffalo with partial paralysis in his face and hearing loss in his left ear.

Mark Ruffalo’s Real Estate Holdings

In 2004, Mark Ruffalo bought a Hollywood Hills home for $1.65 million and sold it to Zoey Deschanel for the same price. He later sold his 19th-century townhouse in Brooklyn for $3.125 million in 2017. There were rumors in 2018 that he and his wife were considering purchasing a $10 million Upper West Side townhouse, but the rumors were never confirmed. The family also owns a home in the Catskill Mountains.
