Interstellar' Movie Plot Spoilers: Aliens In The Film? Scenes On Location In Iceland Include 'Mot

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, May 17, 2024

Christopher Nolan's upcoming film "Interstellar" depicts man's attempts to travel beyond the stars through a 'wormhole.' But is there more to the plot than that?

A report from the shoot location in Iceland mentions a scene that looks like a 'spaceship crash.' While this seems like a major deviation from what we perceive as the plot, it sounds exciting. From a Latino site named Desde Hollywood:

"Desde Hollywood just learned through an inside source that their new location, Iceland, has already seen major action: A Spaceship crash!

Knowing that Nolan doesn't work with a second unit I assume that he directed the sequence created with practical effects. One and that may play an important part in the story.

I am also told that models for some sort of Drones have also been created.

Now, are you ready for the most intriguing part of this scoop?

A bigger aircraft will be featured. Something my source refers to as The Mother Ship."

The site is quick to warn that these are not exactly spoilers and how they arrived at the conclusion of "The mother ship" is still speculation.

However, it is not exactly out of left field for Christopher Nolan to have aliens in his movie even though his only involvement with aliens was writing the story for "Man of Steel."

There is also the possibility that the assumption is inaccurate, and that the so-called "Mother ship" may simply be a larger human ship. Does this indicate a 'migration' of sorts? Did the explorers find another habitable planet and now all the humans are moving?

Hold that thought and remember that the entire post was about a Spaceship Crash!

Spoilers or no spoilers, "Interstellar" is one of the most anticipated films of the year-in large part because of Christopher Nolan (his brother co-wrote the script) and eventually the 'stellar' cast that he attracts to his films headed by newly minted Academy Best Actor Matthew McConaughey, Matt Damon, Jessica Chastain and Anne Hathaway.

All the questions on "Interstellar" will be answered on November 7, 2014. But with the 'Inception' director, the answers only lead to more questions.
