Who is Yakubu Gowon?

Yakubu Dan-Yumma “Jack” Gowon, born on 19 October 1934, is a prominent figure in Nigerian history. He is a retired Nigerian army general and military leader who played a pivotal role in the country’s history during his time in power. This essay will delve into the biography and career of Yakubu Gowon, focusing on his early life, military career, leadership during the Nigerian Civil War, and his life after his rule.

Yakubu Gowon Early Life

Yakubu Gowon, a Ngas from the minority community of Lur in Plateau State, was born to Nde Yohanna and Matwok Kurnyang.

His parents were missionaries, and he spent his early years in Zaria, where he received his education. Gowon exhibited exceptional athleticism during his school years, excelling in sports such as football, pole vaulting, long-distance running, and boxing.

His prowess in sports foreshadowed the determination and leadership qualities that would later define his military and political career.

Yakubu Gowon Career

Gowon’s military journey commenced when he joined the Nigerian Army in 1954, and he was commissioned as a second lieutenant on his 21st birthday in 1955.

His military education included training at the prestigious Royal Military Academy Sandhurst in the UK, Staff College in Camberley, UK, and the Joint Staff College in Latimer in 1965.

His military career saw him participate in United Nations Peacekeeping Forces in the Congo in 1960-61 and 1963. Remarkably, by 1966, he had risen to the rank of battalion commander, still holding the rank of lieutenant colonel.

Yakubu Gowon Religion

Yakubu Gowon, hailing from a Northern Nigerian minority background, is a devoted follower of Anglican Christianity. Renowned for his unwavering dedication to the unity and indivisibility of Nigeria, he stands as a stalwart Nigerian nationalist. Gowon ardently upholds the vision of a united and harmonious Nigeria.

His steadfast commitment to preserving the nation’s integrity has consistently defined his life and distinguished his tenure as a military leader and statesman. General Gowon’s resolute faith in the unity of Nigeria has left an indelible mark on his approach to governance and his significant contributions to the nation’s historical landscape.
